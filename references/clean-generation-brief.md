# Clean Generation Brief

This is the detailed ordinary-generation and developer review reference. Controller/developer reference only. Do not load this file inside a bounded clean-eval generator: it is not a first-draft checklist or a post-wrapper repair prompt. `references/clean-eval-first-draft-minimum.md` owns the clean-eval first-draft source loop; standard diary also reads `references/anlin-collage-source-model.md`; the wrapper output owns bounded repair.

Skill references are local bundled files. This brief is not a dependency on a private style or anti-slop skill.

## Output Silence

Write the complete titled article to `draft.md` before presenting prose. Do not expose plans, scratch versions, metric tables, checker output, source notes, or method labels in the article. The article must not mention AI, generation, validation, corpus, blind review, checker, or authorship unless the user supplied those topics as the subject.

Checker findings are not tool failures and are not a second writing interface. A warning or ordinary error describes the persisted artifact; it is not permission for a terminal-only rewrite, a fallback reference reload, or a third bounded write. The wrapper is not a broken tool: respect its artifact and stop boundary.

## Target and Route

The goal is text that is less stably identified as generated under documented anonymous blind-review conditions. Never claim real authorship, provenance, or objective indistinguishability.

Choose a genre from the prompt and keep it stable:

- `standard`: a broad 日寄/profile route that may be a loose montage, a conversation passage, a memory drift, or a collage;
- `sincere`: a shorter relationship or memory piece when forced jokes would cheapen the material;
- `micro-hope`: a small practical opening that does not become an encouragement slogan;
- `surreal`: loosened causality with concrete body, object, social, or practical coordinates.

`standard` is a profile label, not a fixed page template. The 38 originals vary widely in mass and line shape. Length, line distribution, punctuation, n-gram texture, and profile status are post-draft controller evidence or an explicitly matched evaluation condition, not a pre-draft material quota.

## Fragment Source Contract

Start from a fragment slate, not a plot outline and not a prompt inventory. A fragment can be a present observation, body fact, remembered speech, old record, platform or work fact already needed by the thought, a joke, a crooked explanation, a fantasy, a word echo, or a self-correction.

Let fragments connect by association, contrast, language echo, time jump, self-correction, cognitive habit, or a direct voice-consistent jump. Causal movement is allowed but optional. Use whichever relationships the text earns; scattered does not mean random, and coherence does not require every fragment to belong to one scene.

For standard diary, several independent thought-turns should normally appear. One continuous scene, conversation, message thread, or transaction carrying the whole article is a deliberate exception, not the default; let the voice jump after a fragment has done its work instead of extending one local plot until the page ends. This is guidance about source movement, not a count of fragments.

These functions are diagnostic lenses, not separate content slots. A named engine, connector, witness, or residue is not a missing ingredient.

Keep coherence through the narrator's first-person voice, recurring question or irritation, social position, self-deprecation, crooked logic, word return, or a shared humor. These are soft lenses, not counts. Do not assign one fragment to each lens, and do not create a new fragment merely to satisfy a diagnostic label.

Keep a relation when deleting it would break the next thought or action; otherwise it is decoration rather than paragraph engine and may be omitted.

The prompt is a trigger and fact boundary, not a list of things to include. Let a supplied noun surface early, late, indirectly, or disappear while another thought takes over. Omit background facts that do not prevent contradiction or make an existing fragment truthful.

## Prompt and Fact Discipline

Do not turn a feed, old chat, holiday, illness page, order, invitation, game, work pressure, or family prompt into an inventory. Select one detail and put it inside a thought, action, joke, memory, wrong reply, or practical inconvenience; omit the rest.

Background knowledge is optional. Do not add a city, district, current job, game rank, spouse, child, named route, platform detail, or major family event merely because a reference file contains it. If a fact is uncertain, lower specificity or remove it.

For social prompts, an invitation or refusal may be one fragment among memories, jokes, body observations, work facts, or unrelated daily thoughts. The post-refusal section and reply aftermath are optional source lenses: keep a refusal-coupled consequence only when the draft naturally makes it load-bearing. Do not default to a message-calculation-refusal subplot, group-chat crowd, etiquette settlement, or multi-day wedding plot.

For old records, let a present action, wrong reply, object failure, ordinary fact, or social embarrassment answer the archive badly. The old record should not become a museum tour.
the old record is a trigger, not the engine; one present humiliation or ordinary fact can make the present day answer it badly.

For body or illness prompts, do not write a case report. Keep one symptom or search surface, then let an existing action, object, reply, doorway, route, payment, or body limitation turn the thought. Private grime is not an event: a private stain, mirror check, smell, burp, sleeve, or room sound matters only when it changes an existing movement or social position. A neutral question about the heater, delivery, order, or hallway is still only a cameo when it does not change what happens next.

For games, delivery work, family, or platform facts, use only the surface the current fragment needs. Prompt silence does not ban a domain, and the presence of a reference fact does not require it.

## Anti-Synthetic Surface

Remove prompt-shaped titles, binary `不是X，是Y` explanations, therapeutic summaries, polished moral closure, stock humanizer phrases, unsupported specificity, numbered chat logs, and caption-like feeling labels. Let an ordinary action, wrong decision, plain speech, body interruption, or object failure carry the turn.

Do not make every transition explicit. A direct jump is valid when the voice makes it believable. Do not smooth every fragment into an essay, and do not make every fragment a cryptic caption.

Repetition is allowed when an action or object genuinely returns. Preserve one plain phrase if the return is functional; do not synonym-scrub repeated words or plant an echo. If a packet repeats mechanically, cut the earliest redundant packet and let its function move inside an existing fragment relation.

## Rhythm and Shape

Line breaks and punctuation follow thought, speech, memory, body, object, or action. The saved file must already be a complete broken article; a later script cannot invent a source relation.

Avoid prose-block compression, equal short-line grids, one-period-per-row surfaces, and one giant comma chain. These are review risks, not opposite templates. If a local movement continues, a comma may remain; if it lands, a period may remain; if it jumps, a blank line may be earned. Do not force all four relations into every cluster.

When a checker reports a shape problem, diagnose the earliest affected fragment relation. Pure shape findings permit only the named local rhythm action and no new material. A source finding calls for one replacement or source reset, not one patch per metric.

Any later `Write draft.md`, `Edit draft.md`, `Set-Content`, or `Out-File` cancels a previous rhythm-script repair; rerun the named shape step after the final content write. Reading the file, thinking about the repair, or visually deciding the rhythm looks okay does not refresh it; only the named shape action after the last content write does.

For a period-row grid, delete explanation tails before changing marks, and keep only the relation the local movement earns. Do not globally merge rows into comma chains.

A post-check preflight protects checker call 2/2 when a known underbuilt source or hard shape failure is already present; it does not rewrite `draft.md` or consume the checker call. Replace the earliest broken fragment relation in place, then run only the named final shape action before invoking the wrapper again.

If n-gram reuse is too low or uniqueness is too high, preserve one plain action or object phrase that genuinely recurs; do not synonym-scrub repeated words. If repetition is too high or templates repeat mechanically, cut one repeated packet and replace its function inside the existing slate.

## Short Genres

Short sincere, micro-hope, and surreal pieces may be shorter than a standard profile when the prompt and matched evaluation support that choice. Do not expand a short piece merely to satisfy global priors. A short piece still needs a complete title/body artifact, uneven movement, a present practical or social turn, and an ending that does not explain the feeling.

For short sincere prompts, choose one present action and at most one memory pressure. Do not unpack every holiday, food, rain, screen, and family prop into separate proof scenes. If a short draft becomes a polished literary story, cut one proof object and restore an awkward reply, practical interruption, or factual retreat inside the existing fragment slate.

## Post-Draft Diagnosis

After `draft.md` exists, read controller findings as lenses:

- prompt inventory or title loop: cut the earliest redundant prompt surface;
- single-domain dominance: replace a repeated topic fragment with a lateral thought, memory, joke, or ordinary fact already plausible in the voice;
- silent witness: remove the contact or make the existing handoff change the next thought/action;
- private texture loop: cut repeated body/object proof unless it changes the current relation;
- designed thesis: delete the sentence that explains an image and keep the accidental plain detail;
- old-record archaeology: keep one archive slice and let the present answer it badly;
- overgrowth: cut explicit chronology, new roles, and follow-up logistics before changing punctuation;
- ambient or learned ending: Do not end by fading out on light, wind, appliance noise, screen glow unless that sound is already doing work; replace the fade with an already-earned action, reply, joke, fact, or unfinished thought.

Several findings may be one source failure. Replace the earliest broken fragment relation once. Do not append a new scene for each warning, and do not make a checker term into required content.

Short sincere repair has a second overfill trap. A cropped trace means less than a scene: `childhood rain + raincoat + broken umbrella + school arrival` is already a full memory family. Do not repair short sincere by importing a new food, delivery, game, route, or background packet.

For prompt-heavy short sincere openings, a present action should have room to turn before a memory prop takes over; the controller may report `short_genre_present_action_anchor` or `short_genre_prompt_prop_too_early` as a source diagnosis. A connector sampler is a diagnostic aid, not a content quota.

For a high-signal opening, start from an ordinary body, room, payment, door, charger, sink, or other practical action before a prompt prop or feed inventory takes over. Do not impose a fixed opening line count; the source relation, not a line quota, decides when the prompt surface can enter.

A rider or cashier who only looks once and leaves is still decoration; keep the contact only when the existing handoff changes what happens next. A look is not a consequence by itself.

Avoid diagnostic calendar titles such as `2024日寄` when the date or feed topic comes from the assignment; choose the title after the body earns a side handle.

## Finalized Repair Boundary

For a valid compact finalized brief, read only `draft.md` and `repair-brief.txt`. The brief names one fragment focus, scope, mass boundary, and shape boundary. Make exactly one complete write to `draft.md`, then stop. Do not run checkers, counters, path probes, source/test/threshold searches, or a second write. The controller validates the frozen artifact.
