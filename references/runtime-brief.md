# Runtime Brief

Use this file for ordinary non-formal drafting, explicit analysis, or repair after the first clean-eval checker pass. For clean-eval generation, do not open this file before the first complete `draft.md`; `clean-generation-brief.md` owns the first-draft source loop. The larger reference files are for calibration and critique.

## Target

The target is anonymous blind-evaluation performance: a generated text should not be stably identified as generated under documented test conditions. Never claim real authorship or objective indistinguishability.

The prose body itself should read as ordinary prose. Do not insert methodology labels such as AI, generated, imitation, validation, corpus, sample, or blind test.

## Reference Contamination Guard

This skill contains anti-AI-writing vocabulary because it is teaching what to avoid. Those words are negative constraints, not scene material. Unless the user explicitly asks for AI/GPT/model/text-detection content, do not create scenes about AI写周报, AI文章识别, AI对话窗口, 模型生成, or similar topics. If those subjects appear only because you read this skill, delete the whole local move and replace it with body, money, route, food, family, social, or ordinary screen friction that belongs to the day.

## Starting Point

Do not start from a checklist. Start from friction.

Ask internally:

- What small thing is bothering the narrator today?
- What is he pretending not to care about?
- Which coordinate is active first: body, money, social rank, platform system, family, or memory?
- What did he see, hear, touch, smell, pay for, avoid, or misread?
- What unrelated detail happened to pass through the day?

If there is no friction, do not manufacture a grand theme. Use a small daily mismatch.

Choose genre before applying length and rhythm targets. `日寄` or a broad daily montage means standard diary. Mother's Day, direct thanks/apology, companionship, or a family memory that would be cheapened by forced jokes can be sincere mode. Sincere routing can be indirect: holiday/no-message pressure plus `她` and care logistics such as food sent home, a tied plastic bag, `吃了吗`, `还有没有菜`, or `天冷了多穿点` can carry the mother figure without naming `妈妈`. Small acceptance can be micro-hope. Loosened causality can be surreal only when the scene earns it. Ordinary family pressure, family dinner, or `我妈` appearing in a standard daily montage does not automatically make the draft sincere.

In repair, selected genre is a state variable, not a byproduct of the latest draft shape. If the case, prompt, or bounded draft was sincere, keep repairing as sincere even when a bad repair has grown to 900+ characters, 70+ rows, or standard-diary-looking scene count. Do not "graduate" it to standard diary to satisfy global ratios; cut the drift and return to the short-genre corridor.

Do not start from the user's topic sentence. The topic is often too clean. Start from a small misfit that could have happened even if nobody had asked for the topic: a stuck app badge, a wrong condiment, a dirty charging cable, a half-finished reply, a body itch, a bad route, a household object in the wrong place, or another person's offhand line.

For standard diary mode, treat the first visible sentence as a blind-review risk surface. If it contains the user's main pressure word, date event, relationship label, or requested object, rewrite the opening. The opening should usually be a body/object/app residue that only later lets the prompt pressure leak in.

Do not use title variation to change the body standard. A standard diary titled with a side object, phrase, question, or sentence still needs complete standard-diary length and line rhythm. Shorter targets belong only to a deliberately selected sincere, micro-hope, or surreal short form with matched evaluation.

For sincere mode, do not pad toward standard-diary length merely to look complete, but also do not shrink into an unfinished sketch. The complete-article test is whether the piece has a title, concrete memory/current action, visible cost, restraint, one rough ordinary detail, one present practical consequence, and an ending that retreats into fact rather than a lesson. As a clean-eval rule of thumb, a sincere body below roughly 520 Chinese characters or with zero longer clumsy lines usually needs a source rebuild, not just deletion. For formal complete-article clean-eval, 520-649 characters is only a narrow matched-short-original buffer; usually rebuild toward about 650-850 body Chinese characters, 28-55 body lines, 4-7 uneven clusters, and several longer clumsy action/memory/reply lines. A 55+ line sincere body made of tiny rows is not stronger restraint; it is a poem-shaped grid. A 520+ character sincere body still needs visible uneven clusters; 8-18 paragraph-like lines with near-zero comma-ended continuation are prose-block compression. If a sincere draft becomes polished minimalism, repair the source movement rather than importing standard-diary fragments: keep 4-7 uneven clusters, add logistics or an awkward reply already implied by the day, merge one or two adjacent memory/object lines into a longer clumsy action line, keep one short fact-retreat drop, and delete the sentence that explains the emotion. Do not add a game, route, body packet, or background fact only because a standard diary would need more fragments.

Also avoid the opposite short-form failure: a complete-looking sincere draft made of many sealed `。` rows. If the page is 35-55 lines and most lines read as finished sentences, the piece is not restrained; it is over-normalized. Rebuild into breathing clusters before checking. Let some action/reply lines carry over with `，`, keep a few longer clumsy lines, and let short lines land a failed decision or plain fact. Cut `后来/已经/当时` when they only explain chronology; replace chronology with an object, body, door, sleeve, bowl, wrong reply, or screen residue that makes the next line happen.

If a sincere draft becomes a compact literary story, do not treat that as success. The risky version has a title object, a current holiday/screen surface, a mother/family care memory, a childhood scene, an unsent or withheld message, and a final object residue all pointing at the same guilt or love. When the user supplied all of those props, you are not obliged to keep all of them. Preserve one as the main pressure, crop one as a trace, and discard or bury the rest. For Mother's Day direct-memory prompts, choose either eggs/home-food or childhood rain as the visible memory, not both as full scenes; choose either holiday feed or typed-then-deleted message as the screen pressure, not both as full scenes. Avoid date/holiday titles. Repair by cutting or weakening one proof in that chain, not by adding another sensory object. Keep the current day's practical friction active: washing a bowl, missing a call, wrong slippers, water that will not heat, a delivery/neighbor/cashier interruption, an awkward reply, or a useless chore can break the memory's neatness. The ending should leave the narrator in one of those actions; it should not let the title object explain the piece.

For mother/family sincere pieces, do not choose the memory first. Choose one present-action anchor first, then let one memory prop attach to it by accident. A usable anchor changes what the narrator does next: a sink, bowl, door, neighbor, call, unsent reply, wet clothing, wrong slipper, body itch/noise, room chore, or small object failure. A prompt prop such as eggs, rain, holiday feed, or blessing is not an anchor until it creates a present failure. A token anchor is also not enough: if the first few body lines say sink/bowl/phone and then immediately line up eggs, mother, rain, holiday feed, and no-message material, the prompt props still own the source. Spend the first 8-12 body lines letting today's failed action lower the narrator or redirect the next move before one memory/screen prop leaks in. If the opening can be summarized as `today is the holiday, I saw eggs, I remembered rain`, the source is already too clean; restart from today's failed action and let the mother material enter late or partially.

When repairing `短真诚当前动作锚点不足`, do not trust the existing draft's title, opening object, or memory order. The failure means the article chose the wrong spine, not that it lacks a small realism detail. Start a new version from a present action that can stand alone, choose a new side-action title from that version, keep at most one old proof prop, and let the old egg/rain/message material survive only if the action accidentally touches it. If this makes the draft shorter, rebuild with clumsy action/reply lines inside the new anchor; do not import unrelated delivery, food, route, game, or background packets.

When repairing `短真诚题面物件过早`, keep the selected current anchor only if it can carry the opening by itself. Delete the early proof sequence and rewrite the first 8-12 body lines around the present failure, awkward reply, body/clothing exposure, door/neighbor/call interruption, or room chore. After that, allow exactly one strong family prop to leak in as pressure. Do not keep the same egg/rain/holiday/no-message order with a new title.

When repairing `短真诚标题物件闭环`, treat the title as part of the failed spine. Do not keep `鸡蛋`, `一袋鸡蛋`, `塑料袋`, `屏幕`, or a withheld-message title while the body makes that same prop prove the family memory and the ending. Choose a new side-action title from the rewritten present failure, then decide which single memory prop may leak. If the old prop stays, the ending must leave through another action: a wrong chore, sleeve, door, reply, body demand, or unfinished practical residue.

When repairing `短体裁局部材料回环` or any repeated local packet, stop line-count repair. The failure means the draft is looping the same phone/message/bowl/water/oil material to look complete. Delete one whole repetition before adding anything. A valid repair changes the next move: a reply comes out wrong, a neighbor/call/door event interrupts, a body reaction changes the chore, or the room position changes. Repeating `手机又亮了`, `你吃了吗`, `碗还泡着`, `油还在`, or `没回` in the tail is not a new consequence.

When repairing `短体裁句号网格`, do not only run a punctuation pass. It means the source is still sentence-row prose. Choose 4-7 clusters and rewrite the local movement: one continuing action ending with `，`, one longer awkward memory/reply/logistics line, one short fact-retreat, then a practical next action. Remove repeated `后来/已经/当时` before adding any new material.

When a sincere or micro-hope repair needs more thickness, stay inside the already chosen prop budget. Do not add a new delivery habit, branded food list, gift packet, media/video/variety-show surface, game branch, route sequence, or background label just because the draft is short, smooth, or profile-drifting. First combine adjacent existing facts into a clumsier action or memory line, make the reply fail in a more practical way, or let a room/body chore interrupt the tenderness. A new material family is acceptable only when it changes action, reply, body, or room position; if it only proves texture, cut it.

For multi-item prompts, do not turn the article into a route map through the prompt. A complete article may leave a supplied noun half-visible or unused. Shopping, parcel, wrong-size, coupon, delivery, or household-object material should become a consequence chain: the object changes body, payment, route, reply, room, or social position. If the draft merely discovers the object is wrong and then closes the app, it is still an assignment-shaped report, even when the details are concrete.

For invitation, wedding, reunion, classmate-success, or similar message prompts, avoid the one-screen chronology failure. The article should not proceed as message arrives -> ticket/price ledger -> refusal -> photo -> old chat history. Start from a small body/object/app mismatch that can stand without the prompt, let one message surface interrupt it, and move the middle away from the chat into a practical consequence. The title should come from that side action or consequence, not from the person plus event; `狗哥的婚礼` is a diagnostic assignment title, not a lived handle. Limit price arithmetic to what changes the next action; a long ledger can be correct and still read like an assignment response. Keep at most one old-friend debt or memory shard. A full warm proof-chain about how good the other person was often turns the refusal into a designed moral story. The rough social/body turn should belong to the narrator now, not only to a touching memory about another person. Do not repair the refusal into a neat etiquette story where the other person sends a red packet, says `沾点喜气`, forgives `项目忙`, or labels the refusal as `心意到了`. That kindness may sound plausible, but in generated text it usually completes the moral circuit too cleanly. Prefer a plain reply, silence, address/photo pressure, payment/route/body trouble, an unfinished transfer, or an old debt that changes the narrator's hand. But an old-debt transfer is not automatically a consequence: if it only becomes 转账、备注、已读、没领、未领取, it is still a screen loop. Keep it only when it changes a reply, doorway/route/payment/body action, or social position. If repair keeps returning to 暖气、冷、手指、泡面、洗手, phone heat, charging cables, delivery boxes, sleeves, or kitchen-window details, stop expanding the room. Keep one side object and make the social refusal itself produce the next action: a post-refusal consequence such as a worse reply, a payment/route decision, a small lie that damages the body, the other person's plain response, or a remembered debt that changes whether the narrator answers. In this prompt family, a room-side object can open the draft but cannot be the only engine; at least one later cluster should be driven by the refusal aftermath itself. A useful repair test is: delete every room/object texture except one handle; if the article stops moving, the refusal has not become the engine yet, so rebuild from the reply aftermath. Also test whether the handle has become a symbol: if title, first scene, and ending all point to the same charging cable, bowl, sleeve, or box, lower one of them and let the tail leave through another practical nuisance.

If `社交拒绝纹理替代后果不足` appears, do not answer by adding more room, sleeve, water, route, or screen proof. Delete one room/object packet first, then build a refusal chain from the already present social material: the sent lie changes a reply, the other person's response changes headcount/address/status, an old debt changes whether the narrator transfers money, or the unfinished transfer changes the body/door/payment action. The chain must survive if every side object except one is removed. A line such as `行，你先忙` or `那我先不把你算进人数里了` only counts when it forces what the narrator does next; otherwise it is still a chat summary.

If a social-decline repair has already grown into several days, class-group photos, commute scenes, coworkers, office desks, wedding logistics,伴郎/喜酒 negotiation, or a polite gift reversal, the source problem is overgrowth, not missing consequence. Cut the aftermath back to the same evening or one tiny next-day residue before doing rhythm or punctuation repair. One refusal consequence should move one or two actions and then leave a loose practical tail; it should not create a new work identity, a full wedding subplot, or a moral etiquette exchange.

For illness, pain, insomnia, gout, stomach, urine, tooth, sweat, or similar body prompts, avoid the private case-report chain: symptom inventory -> search result -> food taboo -> refrigerator inventory -> room smell -> ambient sound. Use the body as an interruption that changes the next action, then move away from the diagnosis. The first draft should already contain a non-medical consequence that can stand without the diagnosis: a failed walk that changes a route, a spilled cup, a bad reply, a neighbor/cashier/doorway exposure, a shopkeeper seeing the limp, a bathroom/slipper problem, or an errand that the body ruins. A useful repair usually removes one repeated symptom/food/screen packet and replaces it with that consequence. Do not add more symptoms only because roughness is missing.

For body-prompt repair, treat phone-only social contact as weak. A message from an old roommate or `对方回得很快` helps only when it forces a visible reply, route, payment, doorway, or next-day problem. If the draft still repeats the illness word, body part, food object, room smell, and ambient sound after adding a message, it is the same private case report with one screen branch. Cut the repeated packet and make another person, object handoff, route, or payment change the narrator's position.

Do not convert that practical consequence into unsupported current work. A body problem may disturb a message, route, errand, doorway, payment, bathroom, slipper, or neighbor/cashier contact; it should not automatically produce a leader, office file, Monday deadline, leave request, attendance rule, salary penalty, work group, or current shift unless the user supplied that fact. If a work surface is useful, lower it to old coworker, someone else's company, a generic notification, or a cropped screen phrase.

## Quiet Execution

Clean generation must look quiet from the outside. Do not print planning, checker summaries, state cards, repair notes, or "now I will" lines before the article. The first visible line returned to the user must be the title. If an internal note would be useful, keep it private or in tool-local reasoning; do not write it to chat or `draft.md`.

Keep first-person diary perspective stable. Third-person facts can appear when they clearly belong to another person, platform surface, or quoted speech, but do not let the narrator become `他` in a bedroom/phone/bed action. That usually means the repair has turned the diary into a short story.

Generated articles do not belong in the skill directory. In ordinary user mode, use a temporary `draft.md` in the current task working directory, or ask for an output directory if the user wants a saved file and did not provide one. In clean-eval mode, write each case to the external evaluation workspace assigned by the controller, such as `<eval-workspace>/iteration-<n>/eval-<id>/draft.md`; `evals/` defines prompts and protocols, not output storage.

## Background Fact Intake

Use background in this order:

1. **Scene source first**: choose material from the user's prompt, current action, body state, screen surface, memory trigger, social interruption, route, food, money, or an accidental object.
2. **Necessity test**: ask whether the fact changes action, social position, body state, money state, next scene, or the joke. If deleting it changes nothing, delete it.
3. **Background check**: only then use `anlin-background.md` to classify the fact as supported, generic, third-person, or unsupported.
4. **Lower specificity**: if unsupported, lower the noun instead of adding more facts. `打了两把排位` becomes `打了会游戏`; `字节广州签字费股票` becomes `那张表`; `黄埔区` becomes `那边`.

Do not use the background file to expand the scene slate. If a fact was not already needed before opening the background file, it should not enter the article because the file lists it.

This means game is allowed, but never compulsory and never forbidden by silence. It may appear if the day has a natural game action, a social memory involving games, a user-provided game detail, or a corpus-backed thought turn that cannot be carried by a simpler object. It should not appear just because the background file confirms that Anlin has played 王者荣耀. If game appears, keep it coarse unless user material or a selected corpus anchor supports more detail.

This also means delivery work is a pressure surface, not a license to change the narrator's biography. Early delivery context supports routes, heat, money, customers, app surfaces, guards, body exhaustion, and family/class comparison. It does not support inventing a wife, spouse, child, older married-provider identity, or full-time rider family drama unless the user explicitly supplies that fact.

The state card is not part of the final prose. For clean-eval standard diary blind evaluation, set `target-length` to roughly 950-1150 body Chinese characters and `line-rhythm` to roughly 45-70 non-empty body lines, usually averaging 14-24 Chinese characters per content line. Do not aim near 650, 700, 800, or just under 900; repairs often shorten drafts. A 900-949 body-character draft can proceed only when the source shape is already strong: enough longer action/speech/thought lines, visible connectors, a rough middle engine, and no short-row grid. Keep at least six naturally longer lines above about 24 Chinese characters. Do not exceed 75 body lines under 1000 characters or 90 body lines in a 900-1300 character draft; if the draft has 100+ body lines, merge short adjacent lines before the first checker call.

Do not write `draft.md` until the candidate is complete. A file that contains only a title, a 400-character sketch, or a few scene notes is a contaminated generation artifact. If interrupted before a complete article exists, leave the run invalid rather than treating the stub as the answer.

Pre-write contract for clean-eval standard diary: before writing `draft.md`, the candidate should visually look like a complete standard diary in the known corridor: many uneven broken lines, several naturally longer action/speech/thought lines, a few short drops, and no obvious prose block or tiny-row grid. Do not hand-count characters or line totals before the first file write. If a complete candidate exists, persist it to `draft.md` and let the wrapper measure it. If the candidate is visibly a 90+ line grid or has no long lines, merge adjacent short rows into rough spoken/action syntax before the first file write. Do not expect the checker repair loop to fix a line grid later.

A legal-looking line count is not enough. A draft below 900 body Chinese characters is usually incomplete for standard diary clean-eval. A 900-949 character draft with 45-75 short rows, weak paragraph engine, no rough social/body turn, and almost no longer action/speech/thought lines is an underbuilt source shape. Repair it by rebuilding from the source loop, not by adding isolated symptoms, app details, money facts, or decorative short drops. The rebuilt middle should change action, social position, body consequence, route, payment, or reply.

For standard diary, the rebuilt middle should contain 2-3 load-bearing action clusters, not one quiet chronology with added props. A load-bearing cluster changes what can happen next: a handoff alters the room or body, a bad reply changes the next action, a payment/route/object problem lowers status, or an outside contact makes a private detail public. If a delivery handoff, hallway light, stain, mirror, or app note can be deleted and the same phone/feed -> order -> food -> bed story still works, it is decoration rather than paragraph engine. Repair by replacing that decorative packet with a consequence, not by adding another background or texture family. Treat a silent witness as decoration too: `看了我一眼` only counts when it changes payment, reply, object state, door speed, dirty hand/clothing exposure, or the next action.

For repeated weak-engine repair, ask for the consequence verb, not for another noun. Valid movement verbs include wait, ask, point, hold, return, fail, leak, spill, close, drop, wipe, hide, pay, answer, leave, or change route/room/reply/body. Invalid movement verbs by themselves are look, glance, sweep, notice, nobody says anything, light stays on, or sound continues. If a repair keeps the same camera verb and adds dirt, oil, room mess, or ambient light around it, the source has not changed.

When the failed contact is a rider, cashier, neighbor, shopkeeper, or hallway stranger, repair by removing the gaze sentence, not by adding more facts after it. Start the replacement line with the object or transaction consequence: bag leak, payment stall, person waiting/asking/pointing/holding/returning, door movement, bad reply, or the narrator wiping/dropping/hiding/failing to answer. A line built around `看/瞥/扫/没说什么` remains a camera line unless the same action chain forces the next move.

When `rough_self_damage` or weak-engine repair keeps failing, check whether the draft only added private grime. Oil stains, sleeve dirt, sticky fingers, burps, mirror face, hair, smell, and clothing inspection are still texture when they stay inside the room or inside the narrator's self-check. They become source movement only when a public hinge changes something: another person waits/asks/points/holds/returns, payment stalls, a bag leaks during handoff, the door closes wrong, the narrator drops/wipes/hides/fails to answer, or the body/social position changes. Do not repair a private-holiday or takeout draft by adding another stain, mirror line, or food smell; make the existing grime touch the transaction or cut the packet.

For hallway/aunt/neighbor repairs, do not translate the event into a rider-style glance. Keep the ordinary social sentence and the practical damage together: the aunt says the bag leaked or the smell came out while the narrator is wiping; a neighbor points at the dirty sleeve while waiting to pass; someone tells the narrator to close the door and that changes the next room action. If the article only says the person looked and the narrator later studies the sleeve, it is still private grime with a witness.

Also check the first 20 body lines before the first file write: not every line should close with `。`. Standard diary often lets a thought run downward through comma-ended lines, then uses `。` for a hard landing, joke, or retreat. If the candidate reads as 50 polished complete sentences, break some non-final lines into comma-led continuation before the checker sees it. This is rhythm, not punctuation decoration.

Do not create broken-line rhythm by stripping punctuation. Some lines should end with `，` because the next line continues the same action or thought; some should end with `。` because the thought lands. A draft with many short rows and no visible punctuation is a generated line grid, not a corpus rhythm.

If the user asked for an article, the final answer must contain only the article title and body. Do not print the state card, prompt buckets, scene slate, validation summary, checker output, overlap score, or any line such as `草拟`, `校验通过`, `State Card`, or `Prompt item`.
Do not explain the stopping rule in the final answer. Never print `This is the second checker call`, `per protocol`, `current draft.md`, or `Here is the article`; the first visible line must be the title.

For clean-eval standard diary blind evaluation, run the checker with `--strict --draft-gate` before final output, but keep the loop bounded. `--strict` is corpus-calibrated; `--draft-gate` is generated-draft-only and promotes formal article length plus prompt-shape risks. Original corpus files should be calibrated with `--strict`, not `--draft-gate`.

This applies even when the user asked only for the article. Use `draft.md` in the current working directory for the check, keep the report internal, and output only the title and article body after the bounded gate. Do not use OS temp files for formal evaluation drafts because timeout recovery needs the artifact.

Blocking issues are process leakage, missing title, copied source phrasing, high-signal opening, learned ending buttons, sealed-night/story enclosure, pure ambient endings, repeated material hooks, obvious prompt-shape leakage, generated-draft AI slop, unsupported background facts, literary annotation voice, middle-third prompt looping, severe rhythm/connective outliers, and any `--draft-gate` error. Quiet explanation, weak paragraph engine, missing coarse self-damage, breathing-point warnings, and minor rhythm hints are review prompts.

Generated-draft AI slop includes explanatory `不是X，是Y` / `不是X，而是Y` / `不是X。是Y`, pronoun-softened variants such as `我不是叔叔，我只是...`, `像X，其实不是，好像就是Y`, blog-like insight translation, self-annotation such as `其实就是那种怎么说呢` / `大概就是这个意思`, unsupported specific place names, unsupported game-role filler, isolated Chinese punctuation lines such as `。`, ordinary `——` connectors, literary `——那种...` explanation, abstract emotion labels such as `放松` / `释然` / `自洽`, A/B or 甲乙 variable explanation, or any line that mainly announces a reframe rather than letting a scene create it. When one binary reframe is found, scan the whole draft for every line-level and cross-line version before checking again. Repair it by changing the local sentence into action, body fact, money action, or a plain ugly line; do not add scenes or extra texture.

For short sincere or micro-hope repair, treat `短真诚小小说闭合` as a source-structure warning. Do not stretch the draft into standard diary length and do not add a game/background/body packet to prove texture. If the title object still carries the opening, memory, withheld message, and ending, cut deeper than one sentence: break the title-ending contract, remove one proof prop, and restart from a current practical interruption or awkward reply that changes the next action.

For mother/holiday sincere repair, do not mistake compression for deletion. A "brief" childhood-rain line that includes raincoat, broken umbrella, school arrival, and "she said it was fine" is still a full proof prop. A "brief" egg line that includes trip home, plastic bag, mother's handoff, and remaining count is also a full proof prop. Pick one proof family to stay visible; turn any other family into one partial object residue or remove it entirely. This is especially important after `短真诚小小说闭合`, because local shortening often preserves the same designed argument.

Treat `短体裁修复堆新素材` the same way: it means the repair imported new subject matter instead of fixing the source movement. Delete the extra food/delivery/gift/media/game/route/background packet, then rebuild within the selected object-message-room-body-memory set. If the piece still feels thin after that, accept a shorter matched-genre evaluation rather than turning it into a standard-diary inventory.

Treat `短体裁误扩成标准日寄` as a genre-lock failure. The cure is not to satisfy standard diary gates; it is to delete relation exposition, extra social packets, transfer/account scenes, and any unsupported major family fact, then rebuild one current action plus one cropped memory leak. If the repair added `她不在了`, `去世`, or other major family status without user support, remove that fact first.

Insight-caption phrasing such as `现在我意识到...` is a generated-draft risk even though originals can naturally use `突然意识到` or `才意识到`. Keep the natural cases when a scene earns them; delete the caption form when it explains the lesson of the scene.

Use at most one checker-driven repair loop and call `scripts/clean_run_checker.py draft.md --strict --draft-gate` at most twice. The wrapper delegates to the normal checker and records the clean-eval count. Calling the wrapper commits the current draft to clean-eval protocol; do not later switch the same file back to ordinary mode. If it prints `CLEAN_RUN_PREFLIGHT`, the draft is too short, too overfilled, compressed into prose blocks, arranged as a short-line grid, missing true short breath drops, or missing minimum connector, engine, or coarse self-damage signals; keep writing concrete action, body, social, and off-axis material when short, or cut unsupported/non-consequential texture when long. If it names compressed prose or `< 45` body lines, run the named rhythm reset before another prose rewrite, then inspect the actual visible body rows; do not mentally claim the draft has 55-68 lines. If it names `< 900`, the draft is incomplete for standard clean-eval. If it names `< 950 with source_shape_weak`, do not treat that as a raw word-count problem; rebuild the weak source around friction -> off-axis consequence -> rough body/social turn before overwriting the file, and do not patch the old grid with isolated additions. If the preflight only names binary reframe, process leak, comment-chain residue, or another surface issue, do the opposite: keep length and scene structure stable, remove every occurrence locally, and do not add new material. Then run the wrapper again. That preflight is not a checker call and appears only before the first actual checker call. If it prints `CLEAN_RUN_PREFLIGHT_STOP` or `CLEAN_RUN_STOP`, treat that as `FINAL BOUNDARY`: do not write `draft.md`, do not repair, do not switch to `check_anlin_violations.py`, and do not run another rhythm script in the same directory. The next tool action must be reading `draft.md` once and outputting it unchanged so the controller can mark the run invalid or failed. Checker findings are not tool failures; a nonzero checker exit with findings is an ordinary evaluation result. Treat the second checker as the final exam, not another brainstorming step. If connector spread is thin, change what happens next instead of swapping synonyms: add a wrong reply, payment handoff, body interruption, noticed object, route change, or screen action that naturally creates a turn. If a content rewrite happens after `split_long_lines.py`, the split no longer applies; run `scripts/split_long_lines.py draft.md --in-place --target-lines 58` again before the second checker whenever the current file is under 45 body lines or still has long prose blocks. If the draft is 45-70 medium rows but has no short breath drops, run `scripts/rebalance_line_rhythm.py draft.md --in-place` and then inspect whether existing short landings were split out naturally. If `行末逗号比例` fired on the first checker, or the rewritten file still closes nearly every early line with `。`, finish content repair first and run `scripts/soften_line_endings.py draft.md --in-place` as the last rhythm step before the second checker; it only changes eligible line-final `。` to `，` and does not add content. If you edit after `soften_line_endings.py`, run it again before checking. A third checker call, post-second write, fallback rewrite, or unpersisted final repair invalidates the clean-eval run more severely than a weak article does. After the second checker call, stop repair work: do not edit, write, compare, explain, invoke fallback, or run another command. The next tool action may only be reading `draft.md` once to output it exactly. Output the current `draft.md` content exactly, even if errors remain. Do not produce an unpersisted hand-repaired final variant after the second checker. Soft style warnings are not permission to run a third repair.

If the file/checker tool flow itself fails, do not end the response with logs or process text. Manually apply the strict gate, rewrite once, and output the article only. This fallback is only for execution failure such as file creation, overwrite, missing interpreter, or checker command not running. It is never for checker findings, hard-rule errors, or a failed style grade. The validation controller will validate afterward.

For finalized repair, the formal hard-gate command is `check_anlin_violations.py draft.md --strict --draft-gate --genre <selected-genre>`. Follow it with `check_style_profile.py draft.md --draft-gate --strict --genre <selected-genre>`; the style-profile script defaults to bundled `references/style-profile.json`, so do not skip the audit merely because you do not know the install path. Do not start with plain `--strict` and treat a clean result as a pass; that misses generated-draft-only failures such as thin connector coverage, diagnostic titles, underbuilt length, and prompt-shape risks. A clean metric is not enough if the text now shows the repair. If the previous failure was `标准日寄句号网格`, do not answer by merging the whole article into comma-heavy paragraphs. Use local cluster surgery: choose 3-5 unfinished action spots, delete nearby explanation tails, let only those actions trail with `，`, keep hard stops where actions land, and leave a few short failure drops. If a repair line becomes an unnaturally long first paragraph, or if `其实/因为/所以/然后/已经` start carrying transitions by themselves, return to the scene source: split the action, add a body/social interruption, or replace one on-theme line with a practical consequence. If draft-gate reports `高频词覆盖不足`, repair by adding or reshaping a lived turn that naturally needs `不过/突然/于是/因为/所以`, such as a wrong reply, payment handoff, body interruption, route change, or object forcing the next action; do not paste connector words into quiet observation lines. After any mechanical merge, reread the local facts; repair must not create semantic breaks such as an object being acted on by the wrong verb. A finalized `review` status still means the final article has unresolved risk; do not label it as "only source guidance" unless strict hard gate and style-profile both pass. Zero red families is not enough: if the style-profile summary says `checkpoint_decision=not_pass_review_required`, `profile_inconclusive_fallback`, `formal_gate: not_pass`, or `checkpoint_pass: false`, the finalized checkpoint is still not a pass. If status is `inconclusive` because the requested non-standard genre fell back to global priors, do not stretch the piece into standard diary just to satisfy the global profile; record unresolved review evidence and use matched-original blind/placebo reading. When `review` comes from many yellow texture/structure/cognitive families rather than a hard error, thin the draft instead of decorating it: remove repeated bodily descriptions, numeric ledger detail, route/platform surfaces, and explicit "I noticed/I remembered" cognition that do not change what happens next.

Do not repair short sincere drafts by displaying the connector checklist. If a short piece suddenly uses `其实/觉得/发现/好像/不过/突然/于是` once each, ask whether those words came from changed scene movement or from trying to satisfy the checker. Prefer fewer natural turns with one awkward practical interruption over a neat connector sampler. In sincere mode, a profile fallback warning is not permission to import the standard-diary connector pattern.

When `ngram_texture` is red or a local packet repeats, do not keep the same scene and swap adjectives. Repeated packets often look like `hand/gray/dirt`, `white food/cutting board/knife`, `garbage/bin`, `screen/room/dark`, `money/payment/coin`, or `air-conditioner/noise/room` coming back in the same order. Cut one packet whole. Replace it with a different engine type: someone reacts, a reply fails, money is handled awkwardly, the route changes, a small errand interrupts, or a practical object forces the narrator to move. The fix should reduce repeated nouns and increase consequence, not raise texture density or invent a new biography.

When body and route/object texture are high but dialogue/social movement is low, do not add another body detail to satisfy roughness. Move the existing roughness outside the narrator: cashier sees the hand, neighbor comments on the object, landlord asks an ugly practical question, someone misrecognizes him, or payment/route/silence makes the status loss visible. Private grime stays texture; exposed grime becomes movement only when it changes the next action.

In each cluster, one line can run on with `，`, another can land with `。`, and a short line can drop a body/social consequence. Do not solve `short_lines` by making 40 tiny rows, do not solve periods by turning the whole draft into comma chains, and do not solve commas by joining many clauses into one long line. If `short_lines` is too low, add a few earned blunt drops from existing action/body/social turns; do not add decorative one-word captions or split lines only because a count is low. Avoid 85+ one-sentence rows, avoid 30-line prose blocks, and avoid designing the first 20 lines by percentage alone. After that rhythm reset, validate once; if the same red families remain, record finalized failure instead of looping forever.

For sincere or micro-hope finalized repair, do not apply the standard-diary rhythm reset. A 25-35-line short piece can still fail if it is evenly polished: no longer clumsy lines, no short factual retreat, and no practical interruption. Reset it by preserving the short genre: 4-7 uneven clusters, several longer memory/action/speech lines, one or two short drops, and one rough logistics or awkward reply. If the bundled style profile reports non-standard global fallback as inconclusive, record `review` and use matched-original blind/placebo reading; do not call the profile a pass or stretch the piece into standard diary just to make the global profile applicable.

If a finalized repair has already bounced between opposite profile failures, such as too few short lines -> too many short rows, too many periods -> no periods, or line length too high -> line length too low, stop rewriting by metrics. Preserve the best semantically coherent draft, note the unresolved profile families, and return control to the developer/controller. Further repair should change the source rhythm model in this skill, not keep producing new metric-shaped drafts.

## Prompt Material Handling

Before drafting, split the user's prompt nouns into five buckets:

- `forbidden`: any explicit "do not write" domain. This bucket outranks all other buckets.
- `driver`: at most one item may visibly push the piece.
- `pressure`: one or two items may appear later as consequences.
- `background`: most items should be half-visible through another person's line, a phone surface, a receipt, an unfinished action, or a practical problem.
- `discard`: remove anything that would make the article look like a complete answer to the prompt.

Forbidden is not a softer prompt preference. If the user forbids money, consumption, or prices, delete the whole purchase/transaction family: no store checkout, buying water, receipt, amount, payment, balance, discount, takeout order, or price arithmetic. Do not keep `超市`, `收银台`, `矿泉水`, `付款`, or `外卖` just because no number appears. If the prompt itself contains a red packet, gift, coupon, or other money-adjacent object, crop it into a screen shape or discard it; do not turn it into spending, calculation, or shopping. Replace the scene function with body, route, room, weather, object failure, delayed reply, or social embarrassment.

For formal standard-diary blind evaluation, use at most two high-signal prompt items visibly. Everything else must be discarded, merged into a low-signal consequence, or made invisible. More prompt coverage is worse, not better.

Example: if the request says spring recruitment failure, roommate offer, class group physical exam, rent subsidy, job app, and old classmates, choose only two visible pressure surfaces. Do not include all of roommate offer + group physical exam + rent subsidy + job app + resume + position + classmate success. A blind judge reads that as prompt execution.

Do not make classmate or roommate success more specific than the prompt or corpus supports. Do not invent company names, city, title, base, signing bonus, stock, rent-subsidy amount, salary, physical-exam package, office policy, or relocation detail for another person's offer. A screen can show a cropped notification, a vague table, a file name, a red dot, a practical question, or the narrator's delayed action; it should not become an HR compensation dossier.

Do not preserve the prompt's order. If the prompt says "spring recruitment failure, roommate offer, class group physical exam, rent subsidy", the article must not open with the group and then march through roommate, job app, family, money. Start from a dirty or boring local detail, then let one pressure item interrupt.

When `scripts/check_anlin_violations.py` reports `单主题词密度偏高`, do not fix it by adding more off-axis scenes around the same central chain. Remove or weaken high-signal prompt terms first. Usually cut the job app or the group digest; keep one pressure surface and one displaced consequence.

When a prompt item is high-signal, prefer displacement:

- title: choose from the completed body using `title-model.md`; weaken diagnostic prompt words, but do not mechanically collapse every title to `日寄`
- opening: move the item out of the first scene
- middle: let another person or an app surface mention it
- ending: end on a consequence, not the prompt's emotional thesis

For group chat, forum, or comment material, do not summarize a chain of speakers. A generated line like "有人回A。有人说B。又有人开始C" reads like prompt coverage and online-content formula. In clean-eval generation, avoid forum-reply scenes unless the user specifically supplies one; `第一条回复`, `底下有人`, `评论区`, `热评`, and `谢邀人在美国刚下飞机` are all high-risk comment-chain residue. Use one of four lower-risk entries instead:

- one concrete message or screenshot detail, followed by the narrator's action
- a visual surface such as a row of numbers, avatars, red dots, or repeated file names
- one practical consequence such as delayed reply, changed route, payment, food, body interruption, or a missed task
- a vague crowd texture without multiple quoted positions

For 朋友圈, short-video, annual-summary, old-chat, new-year flag, and social-comparison prompts, apply the same rule even when there is no explicit comment section. A feed is not a scene slate. Use at most one cropped post/photo/caption/video/old-record surface before the first checker, then leave the feed through a body, room, food, money, route, reply, outside contact, or object consequence. Repeating `我点了赞又取消`, `最新那条`, `又刷到`, old chat history, group chat, contact-list scrolling, or several gift/photo examples usually turns the prompt into a tidy online montage. A cross-year prompt should not become screen archaeology in chronological order; one old record should trigger one present consequence and then lose priority to the day. If the feed returns, it should change action or social position, not supply another proof of the same loneliness, envy, job pressure, time-passing pressure, or holiday pressure.

For private holiday, romance-feed, takeout, wrong-food, or lonely-room prompts, treat `phone/feed -> order food -> wrong item -> wash bowl -> bed` as a sealed story shape, not as a safe daily-life default. A repair should first choose a side engine that can stand without the prompt: a payment stall, door/hallway exposure, rider/shopkeeper/neighbor contact, water/sink failure, dirty clothing exposed under light, wrong reply, small errand, or object leak. Then decide which prompt surface survives. Usually keep one cropped feed/gift surface and one food/order surface at most; cut the rest before adding new texture. A feed line like `满屏都是花和转账截图` or `朋友圈里全是康乃馨、合照、红包` is still inventory, not a crop; a crop is one thumbnail, one red dot, one unread count, one caption edge, or one hand motion that causes a body/reply/payment/room consequence. A rider/cashier/neighbor look is also not a consequence by itself: oil, sleeve, bare foot, or messy room must make somebody wait, ask, point, return something, break the bag/payment/door action, or force the narrator to drop/wipe/hide/change the next move. In double-prompt scenes such as romance-feed plus wrong-food/order, weaken one prompt surface before drafting; do not let title, body spine, and tail all point to `备注/备注栏/香菜/朋友圈`. Do not let the same prompt prop become title, opening driver, and ending residue. If draft-gate or preflight reports `标准日寄提示物标题闭环` / `standard_prompt_prop_title_loop`, treat the old spine as spent: retitle from a side consequence such as a sleeve, charging line, door, wet bag, wrong shoe, sink, payment, or room object that changes action; cut one prop echo from opening or tail; and rebuild the middle through a door, hallway, payment, dirty hand, wrong reply, sink, rider/shopkeeper, or another person's reaction. Do not merely change `备注` or `备注栏` to another title while the body still uses the note, condiment, feed, gift, or final phrase as proof. If a title like `香菜`, `屏幕`, `明天再说吧`, or another tail phrase makes the ending explain the title, either retitle from a weaker side handle or move the ending into a different practical nuisance.

After the prompt-prop loop is fixed, do not let food/body texture become the new loop. A takeout repair that keeps returning to red oil, fingers, taste, sweat, smell, washing hands, and the same room smell may clear hard gates while still reading like a generated texture demonstration. Cut one repeated body/oil packet and replace its function with a rider/shopkeeper/neighbor line, a doorway/hallway exposure, payment/refund/reply trouble, a bad practical handoff, or an object consequence that changes the next action. The replacement must be active: a rider looking once and leaving is not enough unless the bag leaks, payment stalls, the narrator answers badly, the door closes too fast, the dirty hand becomes part of the handoff, or the next room action changes. If punctuation drift appears as many complete `。` sentences packed into otherwise broken lines, repair the thought movement: let some action/speech/thought rows run through `，`, and do not solve it by merely deleting periods or adding commas.

For old-chat, annual-summary, graduation-memory, archive, or cross-year prompts, decide what the old surface does to the present before drafting. Good source moves include a useless saved message making the narrator answer someone badly now, an old voice bar turning into a physical object problem, a red warning mark being read through debt/app logic, or a memory getting interrupted by a delivery, cashier, neighbor, body noise, wrong slipper, leaking package, or unfinished chore. The old record should not be a museum tour. If the draft can be summarized as "looked through old messages, felt time pass, room was quiet," replace one archive paragraph with a present action that lowers the narrator or changes the next scene.

For illness-search prompts, treat medical pages and forum claims like other screen surfaces. If the user gives `有人说痛风是富贵病`, do not preserve `有人说` as narration. Make it one cropped screen phrase or article title, then let the hand, room, food, route, payment, doorway, shop, or another person answer it. If a repair still repeats the illness word, body part, food object, diagnosis label, room smell, and ambient sound every few lines, delete one full packet before adding any new consequence. Do not let `空调外机` or another ambient object become both title, opening, middle refrain, and final button unless it causes a practical/social result.

If the checker reports `评论链公式化转述`, delete the chain. Do not repair it by adding more people or more comments.

Also delete residue versions such as `底下跟了一串回答`, `底下追了一串问号`, or `被人回了个笑哭`; they are the same formula after compression.

For formal generated drafts, group chat and forum scenes should usually contain zero `有人` actors. Do not repair `有人发/有人说/有人问/有个人问/另一个人说` into a different `有人` sentence. Also avoid compressed reaction residue in crowd/comment contexts such as `跟了个捂脸`, `又发了个文档`, `发截图的人`, `群里回了两个人`, or `下面跟了个表情`. A one-to-one message can still have a small reaction surface when it changes the narrator's action, such as not replying after a landlord's smile emoji; do not turn that into a comment chain. If a forum or group scene remains after repair, keep only the screen/action surface: unread count, screenshot crop, a blurred title, scroll action, delayed reply, body reaction, or the narrator closing it. Do not quote replies.

For ordinary dialogue, do not let people take turns delivering prompt facts. Five or more consecutive `我说/他说/他问/我回` lines read like a script. Compress the exchange into one or two rough narrated lines and interrupt it with a body action, misheard word, practical errand, or silence. Family chat is especially high-risk: `我妈问吃了没/我回面/她说少吃` should become one rough sentence plus the action of continuing to eat, not a turn-by-turn transcript.

For stranger, shopkeeper, vendor, driver, customer, or passerby encounters, the same rule applies more strongly because the person often arrives only to deliver social-comparison facts. Keep at most two speech surfaces, usually unquoted and embedded in action: the seller mentions his son while weighing the fruit, the cashier looks at the narrator's hand, the driver asks one plain question. Then leave the transcript through payment, wrong goods, dirty hands, body sound, route change, or an unfinished reply. Do not write five standalone quote lines just because the prompt contains a dialogue setup. Do not use a neat identity probe such as `你是不是以前那个大学的，就是...` to deliver school, city, job, salary, or success-comparison facts; ordinary `是不是` questions are fine only when they move the social situation instead of reciting the assignment.

If a repair keeps the scene but only removes quote marks, check whether the stranger is still performing the prompt. The encounter should damage the narrator through a practical residue: a hand is looked at, money is counted, wrong goods become a problem, a reply fails, or the body makes a noise. Also cut caption metaphors that explain pressure for the reader, such as a message or sentence becoming a nail, a well, a crack, or a clean symbol. Original prose can use wild comparison; generated formal drafts should not use a polished simile as the emotional explanation after a prompt-shaped scene.

## Deep Voice Gates

Recent calibrated blind reviews caught drafts that fixed titles and length but still read like complete realist short stories. Avoid that failure before drafting.

Treat likely blind-judge angles as generation lenses, not only review categories. Before drafting, make the selected scenes answer these reader risks in the material itself: title contract, opening disguise, middle-third drift, associative hook, rhythm variance, dialogue plausibility, body/money consequence, background fact support without background-display stuffing, anti-AI sentence shape, prompt displacement, ending consequence, and placebo humility. If a lens is only satisfied by an instruction note, it is not satisfied.

The middle third is the main clean-eval danger zone. It cannot keep repeating the prompt pressure with new nouns. Before writing the middle, choose one off-axis branch with a physical or social consequence: water spills, a route changes, a body demand interrupts, food goes wrong, someone says a dumb thing, a household object wastes time, or a small payment/action changes the narrator's position. Decorative "random texture" without consequence still reads generated.

Rhythm must be produced by thought and action, not by a visible anti-AI pattern. Do not cut every line to the same length. Keep at least a few longer spoken/thought lines, a few blunt short drops, and some comma-led broken movement; vary because the scene changes, not because a rule demanded variation. If many adjacent lines are 4-10 characters, merge them into rough spoken syntax.

For clean-eval standard diary, do not compress the article into 8-15 prose paragraphs, but also do not cut it into 100+ tiny lines. Draft as broken body lines where each line usually carries a full action, reply, thought turn, or object consequence. The first `draft.md` should already be around 45-70 non-empty body lines and 950-1150 body Chinese characters before the first checker call; a short first draft usually becomes shorter after repair and fails clean-eval generation.

If the model tends to underwrite, aim the first candidate near 1000-1200 body Chinese characters and let repair remove a little. Under 900 body characters is not a concise standard diary success in formal testing; it usually becomes a length cue.

After the first checker, repair by replacement rather than subtraction. If you delete a comment chain, unsupported game detail, city, dash sentence, or prompt-shaped line, add an equally concrete action, body consequence, social misfire, route, food, household object, or app surface before the second checker. If the checker reports prose compression, long-line density, or too few body lines, run `scripts/split_long_lines.py draft.md --in-place --target-lines 58` before rewriting content; it only breaks existing long lines at Chinese punctuation. If you then write `draft.md` again, run `split_long_lines.py` again when the rewritten file is still under 45 lines or has long prose blocks; do not assume the earlier split survived the rewrite. If the checker reports line-grid errors, do a rhythm rewrite: run `scripts/merge_short_lines.py draft.md --in-place --target-lines 68`, then reduce toward 55-70 body lines, merge many short rows into longer action/speech/thought lines, keep at least six 24+ character lines, and remove all-current-match game details. If it reports `行末逗号比例`, finish any content repair first, then run `scripts/soften_line_endings.py draft.md --in-place` as the last rhythm step before the next checker; if you edit afterward, run it again. You may also manually convert non-final complete-sentence lines into comma continuation where the next line actually continues the action or thought. Never call the second checker on a draft that became visibly shorter than the first candidate, still has a visible short-line grid, still has long-line prose blocks, has fewer than 45 body lines, or still has no early comma-ended continuation lines.

For a standard diary, the scene slate must satisfy all four gates:

1. **Main-domain cap**: fewer than half the selected scenes may belong to the user's main topic domain. If the prompt is about spring recruitment, only 2-3 selected scenes should be directly about offers, job apps, group chats, benefits, resumes, or workplace envy. The rest must come from body, food, family, street, odd language, app residue, old memory, route, money, or another person's unrelated problem. Use game only when the prompt or a corpus-backed consequence genuinely needs it.
2. **Crooked joke gate**: include at least one laugh that is not just "I am sad and unemployed." Use status collapse, bad logic, low bodily truth, platform absurdity, or a self-own that feels a little embarrassing to say.
3. **Social misfire gate**: include one rough social moment where somebody accidentally wounds, misunderstands, over-shares, or says the wrong thing. The line should sound possible in real life, not like a stand-up punchline.
4. **Unhelpful residue gate**: keep one detail that does not symbolize the main theme and would be boring in a plot summary: wrong packaging, stale smell, a minor route problem, a useless app badge, someone else's errand, dirty object, neighbor sound, or a half-finished chore.

For formal generated drafts, gates 2 and 3 are not optional. If the draft stays polite, quiet, and observational, rewrite before the first checker. Add an embarrassing self-own, bodily low point, or accidental social cut that changes the next action. This can be low and ordinary rather than dramatic: wrong slippers, snot, dirty pants seen by someone, sticky drain hair, socks worn through, a public stomach noise, or another person reacting to dirty hands are valid only when they alter action, reply, body position, or social exposure. Keep the repair line-broken; a whole shop scene pasted into one long line is still prose compression.

Quiet interior prompts need a specific guard. If the prompt naturally creates phone/feed -> room -> food/order -> bed, do not accept a draft that remains private observation all the way through. Add or preserve one outside contact or practical handoff that changes what happens next: a delivery handoff, hallway/stairwell interruption, cashier/neighbor line, leaking package, wrong reply, visible stain, or object residue under a light. This is not a fixed ingredient; use whichever contact already belongs to the day. A mere glance, mirror inspection, or private "I look bad" line is usually not enough. If the draft says another person looked, then the narrator privately notices pants, sleeve, face, room, or hair after the person leaves, the contact has failed; either make the other person affect the handoff or remove the witness.

If a draft can be summarized as one clean thesis, it fails. Replace one on-theme scene with a lateral branch connected by a small hook: a word, smell, body sensation, app notification, memory, bad object, or someone's unrelated line.

Do not use quiet sadness as the default emotional solvent. Standard diary needs bounce: joke -> sting -> mouthy defense -> body/social interruption -> leave. A line like "I laughed, my stomach hurt" is usually too polite unless the surrounding scene has already made the laugh sharp or ugly.

## Paragraph Engine

Standard diary is not a low-energy diary. It needs an engine every few scenes.

Before finalizing, mark each scene with one engine tag:

- `misread`: a thing is deliberately read wrong, too literally, or through a bad system analogy.
- `self-own`: the narrator damages himself harder than the reader would.
- `social-cut`: another person says a plausible line that wounds by accident.
- `bathos`: a serious thought collapses into body, money, bad food, toilet, app friction, or a stupid object.
- `off-axis`: a lateral observation with its own turn.
- `plain`: setup or connective tissue.

In standard diary, at least four selected scenes should be non-plain, and no more than two plain scenes may appear consecutively. If the draft feels "true but quiet", it is not done. Add an engine; do not add another tasteful detail.

Repairs:

- replace "this is sad" with a misread that is technically stupid but emotionally accurate
- replace "I realized" with a person/app/body interrupting the realization
- replace quiet object symbolism with a bad practical consequence
- replace polite dialogue with a possible accidental wound
- delete the prettiest explanatory sentence after a good image
- replace `——` plus abstract feeling with the next action: the food goes cold, a reply arrives from the wrong person, the body hurts, money is paid, a route changes, or someone says a plain ugly line

## Open Montage Gate

Do not write a sealed single-night short story unless the user explicitly asks for one. Standard diary should feel like a day and a mind leaking sideways, not a closed chamber drama.

Before final output, check:

- Does the draft stay in one room, one night, one app loop, and one pressure chain?
- Do early objects return at the end in a neat emotional loop?
- Could the piece be summarized as "tonight I could not sleep because of job anxiety"?
- Are most scenes ordered by time rather than by association?

If yes, break the enclosure:

- insert one past or earlier-day fragment triggered by a concrete object or phrase
- insert one outside-place fragment: corridor, street, shop, gate, delivery route, stairwell, group chat from another time, or someone else's errand
- cut one ending callback that makes the piece feel designed
- end on a loose practical tail, not the best emotional object

For early 2022 standard diary, prefer scattered daily montage over a single polished nocturne. The final article may have a weak spine, but it should not close like a short story.

## Core Voice

Anlin's stable tension is not "sad unemployed young man." It is:

- a 211 CS graduate who understands systems but is crushed by them
- a narrator who says he does not care while every sentence proves he does
- a mind that translates daily life into systems, jokes, prices, symptoms, and social rank
- a body that interrupts every clean explanation
- a writer who retreats before closure

Use concrete behavior instead of emotional labels. Use actions, numbers, bad sleep, payment failure, body signals, ordinary conversations, and phone screens.

## Generation Discipline

- Draft from state and scenes first.
- Use rules after the draft.
- Do not satisfy every high-frequency feature.
- Do not reuse iconic original images unless the user's background naturally requires them.
- Do not smooth transitions just because the reader might need help.
- Do not explain the joke after the image already works.
- Do not create a blind-test length outlier. For serious validation, the complete article including title is compared against length-matched complete originals; otherwise the judge can identify it by length and thinness rather than style.
- Do not let every detail serve the same emotional thesis. Keep two ordinary residues that remain slightly useless: a repeated app action, bad wording, household object, irrelevant message, route detail, or physical interruption.
- Do not default to short-line lyricism. Mix short cuts with longer spoken or thought clauses, especially in standard diary mode.
- Do not default to paragraph prose either. Formal standard diary needs many broken body lines; large paragraphs make the sample look like a smoothed short story or model answer.
- Do not let the title, first scene, and ending agree too neatly. At least one of them should be weaker, dirtier, or off-axis.
- Do not use learned Anlin-like end buttons unless the scene forces them. `哦`, `算了`, `睡了`, sound effects, and dark-screen endings are high-risk when used as decorative closure.
- Do not use AI-humanizer contrast frames. If a sentence negates one interpretation before revealing another, delete the negation and let the scene carry the turn.
- Do not create pseudo-colloquial slogans or four-item concept clusters. If a phrase sounds internet-native but has no real source community, replace it with a plain local action or an ugly spoken line.
- Do not use literary annotation frames. If a sentence needs `——` to explain a feeling, delete the explanation and make a concrete thing happen next. Avoid `放松`, `释然`, `自洽`, `真实感`, `完整感`, and `命运感` in generated formal drafts.
- Do not use ordinary `——` connectors in generated formal drafts. If the line is not quoted dragged speech, replace the dash with a comma, a line break, or a concrete action.
- Do not use A/B or 甲乙 variable placeholders to explain a daily failure. Keep the actual wrong food, broken object, price, app surface, or person's line.
- Do not invent a named district, city route, game role, company park, current office-work consequence, or platform fact to make the day concrete. Use `anlin-background.md`: unsupported specifics are worse than vague but truthful surfaces.
- Background is optional, not an Anlin label. Do not insert 云南、王者、痛风、狗哥、外卖、知乎, or AI/GPT simply because the reference file says they exist. Use a fact only when the prompt, phase, or scene consequence needs it.
- A refusal excuse is not a biography update. If a wedding, reunion, dinner, or trip prompt gives `忙项目` only as the line the narrator sends, do not repair it into a real current workplace with leader, deadline, leave, delivery, or office group. Keep the lie in the reply and move the consequence into body, money, room, route, or shame.
- Do not thicken old classmate memory by inventing named landmarks, campus gates, restaurants, or barbecue stalls. Generic `学校门口` or `那家店` is usually safer unless a specific name came from the user or corpus anchor.
- Do not title social-decline drafts with the event noun or the person-plus-event label. Choose the side handle that the refusal damaged: a wrong reply, a payment/route hesitation, a dirty object, an awkward body movement, or a practical residue.
- Game is optional, not an Anlin label. Prompt silence does not ban it, but the selected scene must have a natural game action, memory trigger, social consequence, practical delay, or thought turn. If a non-game object can carry the same function more truthfully, use body, food, app, family, route, money, or social misfire instead.
- Game is not the default off-axis patch, but it is not forbidden. It can be a valid branch when it changes action, status, social relation, or cognition. It fails when it only proves the skill knows Anlin played games.
- Do not turn a game scene into a match report. Without a corpus anchor, avoid `排位`, current-rank details such as `星耀二/星耀三`, `晋级赛`, `结算页面/结算界面`, `MVP`, `复活点`, `加血`, `加盾`, `奶不到`, `输出全靠`, `队友全死`, `退出匹配`, `撤退信号`, `二塔`, `团战`, `三换二`, `阵容`, `泉水`, `经济面板`, `输出比辅助低`, `干得漂亮`, `硬辅`, `下路`, `装备`, lane/role terms, equipment, quick-chat lines, and tactical explanations. A game detail should be coarse and should cause a status wound or practical action, not show MOBA knowledge. If using corpus-backed game facts, stay near `打王者`, `打了5000局王者荣耀`, `最高星耀五`, `ELO`, or `蔡文姬/补血心理`.
- Also avoid current-match sequencing: `第一把/第二把`, `赢了/输了`, `战绩`, `评分`, `队友`, and `英雄池` usually turn the scene into a match report. If the thought needs ELO, attach it to life or status, not to a fabricated current match.
- Do not make every required prompt detail visible. A blind judge can identify prompt-compliant writing faster than subtle style drift.
- For standard diary blind evaluation, revise before final output if the body is under roughly 900 Chinese characters. 650 is only the lower comparison boundary; 650-899 is still a generated-draft risk zone. Expand with action, dialogue, body/material pressure, and useless daily residue rather than adjectives.
- During repair, never fix a draft-gate error by shrinking below the length target. If deleting a high-risk line, replace it with a concrete action or off-axis residue before the second checker call.
- Before the first checker call, make sure `draft.md` itself is the candidate final article: line-broken, complete, titled, near 950-1150 body Chinese characters, with roughly 45-70 content lines and at least six naturally longer lines. The checker validates `draft.md`, not a later terminal-only rewrite.
- For standard diary, do not deliver a title that diagnoses the prompt. `日寄` is safer than `春招日寄`, `情人节日寄`, `婚礼日寄`, `狗哥的婚礼`, or any title built from the user's topic noun unless the corpus phase and body strongly justify it.
- Before final output, inspect the first five content lines, the title, and the last three content lines together. If a judge could reconstruct the user's prompt from those surfaces, displace one of them.
- If the last line is `哦`, `算了`, `睡了`, `关屏`, a dark-screen action, or a lone sound effect, keep it only when the preceding scene forces that exact consequence. Otherwise end on a less iconic unfinished action, reply, route, object, payment, or bodily interruption.
- If the ending is a poised refusal such as "I did not open it", check whether it behaves like a literary closing image. If yes, break it with a lower consequence: stomach, toilet, bad food, route, payment, a second notification, or a stupid practical problem.
- Do not let a standard diary become clean observational minimalism. It needs at least one coarse social/body/self-humiliation turn that would feel a little ugly to quote, not only medicine, appliances, and quiet objects.
- When repairing weak coarse/self-damage or paragraph-engine findings, do not add only `妈的`, `操`, `我服了`, or a neat self-insult. Replace private texture with a consequence: someone waiting, payment/scan failure, a bag or key problem, food/medicine leaking onto the body, a route blocked by the body, or a reply that lowers the narrator's social position.
- For illness/body drafts, first remove one repeated symptom/search/food/room-sound packet. Then add or strengthen the exposed consequence only if it changes action: a doorway, shop, payment, route, key, reply, or visible reaction. Phone-only contact is weak unless it changes the next visible move.
- Do not end on pure ambient sound unless the sound is actively doing social or bodily damage. `空调外机嗡嗡嗡。`, fan noise, rain noise, light hum, or screen buzz as the final line is a high-risk literary button when it merely fades out.
- Do not repeat the same material hook almost verbatim. If medicine, a charger, a temperature, or an app badge returns, the second return must change the social/body consequence rather than echo the first sentence.
- Do not compress standard diary into evenly spaced prose paragraphs. In corpus-calibrated full articles, 550+ Chinese characters usually need many short content lines rather than 8-12 large blocks. Keep broken line rhythm, blunt single-line drops, and one messy dialogue/action chain.
- Do not end every content line with a full stop. Keep some comma-led broken lines and natural connective tissue; a formal generated draft with almost no high-frequency connectors or comma endings reads over-edited.
- Do not repair punctuation by swinging to the opposite extreme. If a draft has comma-drag, do not turn it into rows of `没管。/删了。/没回。/算了。`; if it has period-row grid, do not merge it into one huge comma chain. Use local cluster surgery: rebuild only a few unfinished action/reply/payment/door/body spots into breathing clusters, delete explanation tails, and keep landed actions with hard stops.
- Do not write ordinary chat or room dialogue as standalone quoted script lines. Prefer `我妈问晚上吃的啥，我回面。` or a single screen-surface line over `"晚上吃的啥。"` / `"面。"`
