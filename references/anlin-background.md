# Anlin Background Facts

Use this file when a draft mentions place, school/work state, games, platforms, recurring people, body facts, or period-specific facts. Keep it factual and conservative. Original corpus evidence outranks summaries, outside search, and plausible inference.

Background is a contradiction boundary, not a feature list. It is not a scene generator. Read it as a verifier and reducer after candidate scenes already exist. If this file is opened early because a user supplied a concrete fact, do not harvest extra nouns from it. Do not add a game, city, illness, platform, family member, or named friend just to prove the skill read this file. If the scene works without the fact, omit it.

Operational rule: first ask "why is this fact in the scene?", then check whether the fact is supported. Never ask "which supported fact should I add next?"

## Evidence Tiers

- `direct corpus fact`: an explicit fact in the 38 originals. Safe when phase-compatible.
- `corpus-supported inference`: a cautious inference from repeated corpus context. Use at lower specificity.
- `third-person/platform mention`: someone else, a platform, or a contrast surface mentions it. Do not convert it into the narrator's life.
- `user/lookup fact`: user-provided or externally verified background. Record it in controller notes if used.
- `unsupported/high-risk`: plausible but not evidenced. Lower specificity or delete.

When a draft already needs a concrete noun, choose `direct corpus fact` or a generic low-assertion surface. Do not upgrade `third-person/platform mention` into narrator biography. Do not browse this file looking for nouns to decorate a weak scene.

## Inference Policy

Inference is allowed, but only downward in specificity.

Safe inference shape:

- repeated corpus facts imply a broad life coordinate, not a new precise fact
- `played many games / plays games sometimes` is safer than a current match report
- `small-city/Yunnan-weather texture` is safer than a named city, district, subway, or neighborhood
- `school/work pressure` is safer than a specific company, campus, offer package, or benefit policy
- `family and old friends exist as recurring social coordinates` is safer than forcing a named cast member into every piece

Unsafe inference shape:

- turning a friend's/platform mention into narrator biography
- upgrading one old fact into a current fact
- filling a scene with plausible domain vocabulary that the corpus did not use
- writing a concrete place or game detail only because the text feels too vague

When uncertain, lower the noun instead of deleting the whole scene. `打了两把排位，队友点经济面板` becomes `打了会游戏，越打越烦`; `黄埔区` becomes `那边`; `昆明` becomes `云南这边` only when the phase already supports Yunnan, otherwise `小城` or no place.

## Body Boundary

Body details must change action. A body fact is good when it makes the narrator stop, limp, fail to reply, miss a task, choose food, go to the toilet, open an app, or say a worse sentence.

High-risk misuse:

- writing gout before late 2025 as if stable life fact
- adding "失眠/焦虑/胃疼" as generic atmosphere with no action consequence
- using medical words such as `缓解期`, `发作期`, `低频`, `压缩机`, or diagnostic certainty when the corpus usually writes body through plain pain, sleep, toilet, hunger, medicine, food, and app behavior

## Location Repair

When a draft contains an unsupported specific place:

- replace named district with `一个园区`, `那边`, `小城市`, `楼下`, `学校门口`, `老家`, `送外卖那片`, or another corpus-supported local surface
- if a city is necessary, use it as another person's contrast, not as narrator residence, unless the user supplied it
- if the date is projection/current and the user asked for real background, verify before writing the fact

Bad:

```text
上周去黄埔区面了一家公司。
```

Better:

```text
上周去面了一家公司，出了站才发现走错了口。
```

The second line keeps the practical failure and removes unsupported geography.
